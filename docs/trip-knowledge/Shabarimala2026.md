Source: sabarimalaayyappan.org. This is the tentative schedule / wishlist for the group pilgrimage.

## Mandala Vratham Start Date

30th June / 7th July is the start day (48 / 41 days) for the austerities. In Kerala the Vratham is 41 days before Irumudi; in Karnataka it is 48 days. Some people complete the 41-day Vratham after returning from Sabarimala.

## Things to Do Before Leaving the House

1. Make sure all items are packed as per the checklist.
2. Take a bath, wear the black dhoti, drape the shalya over your shoulders, and apply tilak.
3. Collect a handful of rice from each family member, including children.
4. Offer prayers to your house deity and Guru; seek blessings of parents and elders.
5. Light two ghee lamps in the pooja room.
6. Perform Mangala Aarathi with camphor, praying for the Lord's divine darshan.
7. Offer namaskaram to your house deity and elders.
8. As per custom, leave the house silently without formal goodbyes — inform your family of this tradition in advance.

## Things to Do After the Sabarimala Pilgrimage

1. The Mala should be removed by the Guru Swamy at the same place where it was adorned — by the temple priest if worn in a temple, or by the Swamy himself if worn personally.
2. Family members may wash your feet and perform Aarathi before welcoming you home. If the Mala was worn at home, remove it reverently at home.
3. Place the Mala in the pooja room as a sacred offering or preserve it safely for future pilgrimages.

## What to Do If You Are Lost

Always stay with the group. If you are separated and cannot reach team members by phone, do not panic — wait at the designated location:

- At Guruvayur: wait in front of the temple's main door.
- At Thrissur: wait at the main entrance of the Vadakkunnathan temple.
- At Chottanikkara: wait at the entrance of the main (upper) temple.
- At Pampa: mobile coverage is poor (usually only BSNL works). While going up, wait near the start of the steps at Pampa Ganapathy temple (just before the Virtual Q / Aadhaar check). While returning (worst case), wait near the Indian Oil petrol bunk.
- At Sabarimala: wait in front of the Holy 18 Steps if you are below the main temple, or near the Melshanthi room if you are on top.
- All other temples: wait at the main entrance of the temple.

## 108 Ayyappa Sharanam

The group chants the 108 Ayyappa Sharanams, each starting with "OM" and ending with "SARANAM AYYAPPA" (e.g., Swamiye, Harihara sudhane, Kannimoola Maha Ganapathy bhagavane, Maalikaippurattu Manjamma Devi, Vaavar Swamiye, Sabari giri vaasane, and so on up to Patinettaam padikkudaiyanaadhane). Closing prayer: "Swamiye Sharanam Ayyappa."

## How to Observe Mandala Vratham

Devotees begin the vratham by wearing a Tulasi or Rudraksha mala. After this, male and eligible female devotees (girls aged 1–12 and women above 55) are addressed as Ayyappa Swamy / Swamy and Maalikapuram until the pilgrimage is complete. Devotees wear only black clothing. Kanni Swamis should get the mala worn by a priest at the temple.

Vratham guidelines:

1. Follow simple living and maintain absolute cleanliness.
2. Complete abstinence from alcohol, tobacco, betel leaves, and non-vegetarian food.
3. Do not cut hair or trim nails during the vratham period.
4. Bathe at least twice daily (preferably thrice); chant "Ayyappa Sharanam" at least 108 times and remember Lord Ayyappa continuously.
5. Cause no verbal or physical harm to anyone.
6. Treat all fellow devotees as Lord Ayyappa and serve them with respect.
7. Remain humble; dedicate all respect received to Lord Ayyappa.
8. Do not neglect family responsibilities during the vratham.
9. Treat each year's vratham as the first time, with strict adherence.
10. Follow strict brahmacharya; maintain pure thoughts and respect towards all women.
11. Do not apply oil to hair or body during bath.
12. Avoid social events (birthdays, weddings, engagements, feasts) at non-vratham homes.
13. Avoid contact with a dead body; if it happens, bathe before continuing.
14. Carry a Tulasi leaf for spiritual protection and purity.
15. Simple living: sleep on a mat (not a bed), no pillow, no footwear, and no umbrella.
16. Complete surrender to Lord Ayyappa is the essence of the vratham.
